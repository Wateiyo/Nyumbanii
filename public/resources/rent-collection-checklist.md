## üìÖ Monthly Collection Process

### Week Before Due Date (e.g., Last week of month)
- [ ] **Send payment reminder**
  - 7 days before due date
  - Include amount, due date, and payment methods
  - Use Nyumbanii's automated reminder feature

- [ ] **Review tenant payment history**
  - Check for previous late payments
  - Note any payment arrangements
  - Update contact information if needed

### On Due Date (e.g., 5th of month)
- [ ] **Monitor incoming payments**
  - Check M-Pesa messages
  - Review bank account transactions
  - Update Nyumbanii payment records

- [ ] **Issue receipts immediately**
  - Send via SMS/email within 24 hours
  - Include payment date, amount, and balance
  - Keep digital and physical copies

- [ ] **Update payment status**
  - Mark as "Paid" in Nyumbanii
  - Note payment method and reference
  - Update tenant account balance

### 1-3 Days After Due Date
- [ ] **Contact tenants with unpaid rent**
  - Friendly reminder via SMS/call
  - Verify if payment was made but not recorded
  - Ask about any payment difficulties

- [ ] **Check for partial payments**
  - Record partial amount received
  - Communicate remaining balance
  - Set payment plan if necessary

### 4-7 Days After Due Date
- [ ] **Send formal late payment notice**
  - Reference lease agreement terms
  - State late fee if applicable
  - Provide new payment deadline (e.g., 2-3 days)

- [ ] **Calculate and add late fees** (if applicable)
  - Follow lease agreement terms
  - Document in tenant account
  - Include in next communication

### 8-14 Days After Due Date
- [ ] **Schedule in-person meeting** or call
  - Discuss payment challenges
  - Explore payment plan options
  - Document agreed arrangement in writing

- [ ] **Send payment plan agreement**
  - Written terms of arrangement
  - New payment schedule
  - Both parties sign

### 15+ Days After Due Date
- [ ] **Send final notice before legal action**
  - Reference all previous communications
  - State consequences of non-payment
  - Provide final deadline (e.g., 7 days)

- [ ] **Consult legal advisor** if needed
  - Review lease terms
  - Check local landlord-tenant laws
  - Prepare eviction notice if required

## üÜò Emergency Situations

### Tenant Claims Payment Was Made
1. Check all payment channels (M-Pesa, bank, cash log)
2. Ask for proof of payment (transaction ID, receipt)
3. Verify with payment provider
4. If confirmed, apologize and update records immediately
5. If not found, request tenant to resend or make new payment

### Tenant Loses Job/Emergency
1. Listen with empathy
2. Review lease terms for hardship provisions
3. Offer realistic payment plan (e.g., 50% now, 50% in 15 days)
4. Put agreement in writing
5. Monitor compliance strictly

### Multiple Tenants Late Simultaneously
1. Check if there's a system issue (e.g., M-Pesa down)
2. Send mass communication about alternative payment methods
3. Extend grace period if issue is beyond tenant control
4. Process individual cases fairly
5. Learn from incident to improve payment system

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Set rent due early in month** (5th instead of 1st) - Gives tenants time after month-end salaries

2. **Accept partial payments wisely** - Only if there's a clear plan to pay balance

5. **Review annually** - Update lease terms, payment methods, and late fees based on experience
